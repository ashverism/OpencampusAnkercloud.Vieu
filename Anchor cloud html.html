What Is Vieu

Vieu is not a lead generation tool. It is an intelligence layer built on 9 billion executive relationships that tells your team which accounts to pursue, why now, who to talk to, how to reach them through your existing network, and what to say when you get there. The output is structured to be immediately actionable by your sales team without additional research.

What the Graph Indexes
People, Companies, and the Signals Between Them
Vieu pulls from 40+ public data sources and enriches across three layers:
  • + Professional history and current roles
  • + Social media interactions: shared posts, comments, likes
  • + Conference panels and speaking appearances together
  • + Co-filed patents and published research
  • + Alumni and shared company tenure
  • + News mentions, press coverage, and public announcements
  • + Job postings, technographic signals, and company filings
Relationship Scoring
Strength, Not Just Existence
Every connection in the graph is scored for relationship strength based on frequency, recency, and type of interaction. A warm path where two people worked together for two years and have interacted publicly multiple times ranks higher than a one-time connection. The strongest paths surface first.
Applied to GTM
From Data to a Door
Given any target account and any target stakeholder, Vieu finds the shortest, strongest path from your combined network to that person. That path comes with the context to make the introduction credible: what connects the two people, when they last interacted, and what to reference.

GTM Priorities: Next 90 Days

The goal is logos and ACE pipeline. Every priority below is sequenced to move that outcome forward as fast as possible, with the 60-day ACE milestone as the primary forcing function.

  • Lead with the joint solution into higher education
    The confirmed Ankercloud and OpenCampus joint delivery on AWS Bedrock is the opening credential. University CIOs evaluating AI-first campus modernization do not need to be convinced the solution works. They need a reason to take the meeting. The AWS case study, the 152 live campuses, and the infrastructure-plus-application pitch is that reason. Every outreach into a new university account should anchor on this.
  • Activate the AWS partner network as the primary warm-path source
    The strongest shared warm paths into university CIOs are not personal LinkedIn connections. They are AWS higher-ed and Bedrock partner managers who already work with both Ankercloud (as an AWS Premier partner) and OpenCampus (as a Bedrock customer). A single AWS contact can unlock both relationships simultaneously. Vieu maps this network in week one and surfaces the highest-confidence paths first.
  • Run Ankercloud's enterprise and public-sector motion in parallel
    The joint higher-ed story is the flagship, but Ankercloud's standalone enterprise motion is a parallel track that does not depend on OpenCampus timing. Cloud migration, agentic AI governance, and sovereign cloud deals in healthcare, manufacturing, and public sector have their own buying group and their own entry conversations. Both tracks contribute to the 10 ACE opportunity target in 60 days.
  • Use OpenCampus reference customers as peer introduction nodes
    King's College London, Karl Landsteiner University, and Catholic University of Eichstatt-Ingolstadt are not just proof points. They are network nodes. CIOs talk to each other. Vieu maps the relationship paths from these reference accounts into comparable institutions in EMEA and North America so the team can approach new prospects with a peer-validated introduction rather than a cold ask.
  • Target Ellucian and Workday Student displacement accounts specifically
    Rather than waiting for inbound interest, build a portion of the target list around institutions with identifiable SIS contract pain: Ellucian renewals approaching, Workday Student implementation delays, or institutions publicly citing modernization in strategic plans. These accounts have a concrete reason to evaluate an alternative. Vieu surfaces technographic signals and contract tenure data to identify them.
  • Activate the EDUCAUSE network for North America expansion
    OpenCampus's EDUCAUSE Nashville presence in 2025 is the beachhead. The EDUCAUSE community contains the exact CIO and IT leadership personas that are hardest to reach through standard outbound. Vieu maps this network and surfaces warm paths from the combined team's existing connections into EDUCAUSE community members, chapter leaders, and conference speakers who can open doors at target institutions.
